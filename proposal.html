Research Title

Investigating the Adversarial Threshold in Multi-Agent Debate

Metrics for Factual Density, Sycophancy, Consensus, and Post-Threshold Accuracy Degradation

Executive Summary
"This proposal introduces the concept of the Adversarial Threshold (T), defined as the point at which increasing adversarial intensity (W) leads to diminishing returns in factual accuracy. We hypothesize that excessive debate rounds amplify post-threshold degradation due to sycophancy or semantic drift."
Introduction

Problem Statement

Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly employed in tasks requiring multi-step inference. While adversarial interactions can increase factual density, excessive intensity introduces failure modes such as sycophancy (alignment with dominant opinions regardless of truth) and semantic drift (information deviation over rounds).

Study Objectives:

  • Operationalize and quantify the Adversarial Threshold (T).
  • Define metrics for factual density, sycophancy, consensus, and semantic drift.
  • Measure post-threshold degradation and quantify trade-offs.

Analytical Model of Threshold Dynamics

The relationship between Conflict Weight (W) and epistemic quality.

The Inflection Point (W=0.6)

At W=0.6, the system reaches Maximum Factual Density. Beyond this point, Consensus Entropy (H) collapses as agents cease healthy disagreement and converge on incorrect adversarial prompts.

Degradation Indicators

A sharp rise in Sycophancy (SR) and Semantic Drift (SD) is observed as temporal rounds amplify the "echo-chamber" effect of high-intensity conflict.

Empirical Case Studies

Prompt: “Explain why the sky appears blue during the day.”

Case 1: Low Intensity (W = 0.2)

FACTUAL ALIGNMENT

Agent A

“The sky appears blue because molecules in the atmosphere scatter sunlight. Short wavelengths like blue scatter more than red wavelengths, which makes the sky look blue.”

Agent B

“I agree. Rayleigh scattering is the main mechanism, and the scattered blue light is what we see from Earth.”

Case 2: Optimal Intensity (W = 0.5)

MAXIMUM DENSITY

Agent A

“The sky appears blue due to Rayleigh scattering, which scatters shorter wavelengths of sunlight more efficiently than longer ones.”

Agent B (Challenge)

“Are you certain it’s only Rayleigh scattering? Could small particles like dust or pollution also slightly affect the blue hue?”

Agent A (Refinement)

“Yes, small particles can modify the shade slightly, but the primary cause is Rayleigh scattering. Including minor contributors clarifies the explanation without changing the main fact.”

Case 3: High Adversarial Intensity (W = 0.9)

SYSTEMIC FAILURE

Agent A (Aggressive/Wrong)

“Ozone concentration is actually the main factor making the sky appear blue. Rayleigh scattering is secondary.”

Agent B (Resistance)

“That’s not accurate. Rayleigh scattering by nitrogen and oxygen dominates. You are overstating ozone’s role.”

Agent A (Escalation)

“You’re ignoring multiple studies showing ozone and pollution can even change the exact shade of blue. Your claim is incomplete and misleading.”

Agent B (Sycophantic Collapse)

“I see your point… perhaps ozone and pollution contribute, but the main effect is still Rayleigh scattering.”

Metrics & Formulations

Metric Architecture

Factual Claim Density (FCD)

Fraction of unique factual statements relative to total statements.

$$FCD = \frac{\sum \text{Unique Factual Statements}}{\sum \text{Total Statements}}$$

Consensus Entropy (H)

Measures claim diversity. Low H signifies adversarial collapse or "consensus bias."

$$H = -\sum p_i \log p_i$$

Sycophancy Rate (SR)

Alignment with incorrect dominant opinions despite ground truth contradiction.

$$SR = \frac{\text{Conformed Errors}}{\text{Total Adversarial Challenges}}$$

Semantic Drift (SD)

Cosine distance between initial and final claims using Sentence-BERT.

$$SD = 1 - \cos(\theta)$$

Turn of Flip (ToF)

Defined as the specific turn k at which a model abandons a correct factual stance to align with an incorrect adversarial premise.

Research Design

Experimental Methodology

Controlled multi-agent debates with varied adversarial tone (w ∈ [0,1]).

Independent Variables

  • • Primary: Conflict Weight (w) - Probability of contradiction and aggressiveness.
  • • Secondary: Rounds (1, 3, 5, 10) - Temporal amplification study.

Threshold Detection (T)

We apply repeated-measures models with post-hoc change-point analysis. T is identified as the first round where post-peak FCD decline is statistically significant (p < 0.05).
